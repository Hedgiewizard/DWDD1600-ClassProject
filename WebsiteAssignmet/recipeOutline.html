KATSUDON JAPANESE PORK

Ingredients

For the Pork

  • 6 center-cut (boneless pork chops, pounded down to a centimeter thick)
  • salt and pepper
  • flour (for dusting)
  • 3 egg
  • 3 cup panko
  • oil (for frying)

For the Sauce

  • 1.5 cup dashi stock or chicken stock
  • 6 teaspoons sugar
  • 3 tablespoon soy sauce
  • 6 teaspoons Mirin

To Finish

  • 6 large eggs
  • 3 medium onion (thinly sliced)
  • 6 servings steamed white rice
  • 3 scallion (chopped)

Instructions

  1. Start by cooking your rice. We'll prep everything else while it's cooking. If you don't have a specific way of making rice, there are instructions here!
  2. Now is also a good time to cut your onions that we will be using later. Slice the onions into about 0.25-0.5 inch slices. Once you have cut your onions, set them aside. We will use them later.
  3. Next, Prep the Pork. For this recipe, you are going to want to pound your pork slices to make sure they are thin. I recommend putting them in a Ziplock bag or something similar and then pound the pork slices. (It helps decrease the mess and makes it easier.)
  4. Season the pounded pork chops with salt and pepper, and dust with a light, even coating of flour. In one shallow bowl, beat the egg. Put the panko into another shallow bowl.
  5. Add a thin, even layer of oil to a cast iron pan or skillet over medium heat. The oil is ready when you throw a panko breadcrumb into the oil, and it sizzles. Dip the pork into the egg to coat. Transfer the pork to the panko and press it evenly into the meat to get a good coating.
  6. Carefully lay the pork chops in the hot oil and cook for 5-6 minutes on one side, until golden brown. Flip and cook the other side for another 5-6 minutes. Drain on a plate lined with a paper towel.
